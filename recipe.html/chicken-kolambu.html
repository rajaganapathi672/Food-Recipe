Chicken Kolambu (South Indian Style)

Prep Time: 25 minutes

Cook Time: 40 minutes

Servings: 4

Ingredients:

  • 500g Chicken (bone-in preferred)
  • 2 Onions (sliced)
  • 2 Tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp Ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp Oil (preferably gingelly oil)
  • 1 tsp Mustard seeds
  • Few Curry leaves
  • 1 tsp Red chili powder
  • 1 tsp Coriander powder
  • ½ tsp Turmeric powder
  • ½ tsp Garam masala
  • Salt – to taste
  • 1 cup Water (adjust as needed)
  • Chopped coriander for garnish

To Grind (Masala Paste):

  • ¼ cup Grated coconut
  • 1 tsp Fennel seeds (sombu)
  • 5 Cashews or 1 tsp Poppy seeds (optional)
  • A little water

Instructions:

  1. Grind the coconut, fennel, and cashew into a smooth paste. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a kadai. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add curry leaves and sliced onions. Sauté till golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste. Fry till raw smell goes away.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes. Cook till they turn soft.
  6. Add turmeric, chili, coriander, and garam masala powders. Mix well.
  7. Add chicken pieces and salt. Sauté for 5–6 minutes till chicken changes color.
  8. Add water, cover, and cook for 20 minutes or till chicken is tender.
  9. Add the ground masala paste and simmer for another 5–10 minutes.
  10. Garnish with coriander and serve hot with rice, dosa, or idli.
